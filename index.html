What is the S&P 500?

The S&P 500 is an Index that tracks the performance of 500 of the largest U.S. companies. It covers about 80% of the total market cap of U.S. equities and is often used to measure the performance of the entire American stock market.

Tracks 500 of the largest U.S. companies.

Why trade the S&P 500?

The "Basket of Fruit" Concept

Instead of buying one apple (a single stock), you get a whole basket containing small pieces of 500 different fruits.

Safety

If one fruit goes bad, the rest of the basket can stay fresh.

Growth

If the whole farm has a great season, your basket increases in value.

Ease

You get instant exposure across all major market sectors.

What makes it popular?

Diversity

Broad exposure reduces single-stock risk.

Security

Reviewed and updated by a standard committee.

Historic performance

Long-term average returns around ~10% per year.
